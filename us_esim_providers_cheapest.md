# USA eSIM Providers — Real US Number with SMS / Wi-Fi Calling (July 2026)

## User's Goal
- US phone number via eSIM (real US number, not VoIP virtual)
- Global support / Wi-Fi calling and messaging
- **No data access requirement** — SMS + calls only is fine
- Sorted by cheapest price
- App-hosted eSIM is OK (don't need physical SIM shipment)
- US mainstream carriers (T-Mobile/Verizon/AT&T) and non-US providers both OK

## Tier 1 — Cheapest (Real US Numbers, eSIM, Wi-Fi Calling)

### 1. TextNow — **$0/month (FREE)**
- **URL:** https://www.textnow.com
- **Plan:** "Free Flex Plan" — $0/month, unlimited talk & text over Wi-Fi, free eSIM on iOS/Android.
- **Number type:** Real US number (TextNow is a US MVNO riding T-Mobile's network — numbers are allocated from TextNow's NPAC-registered pools).
- **Wi-Fi calling:** Yes — primary mode for free plan. Works over any Wi-Fi or hot-spot globally.
- **SMS:** Unlimited free SMS/MMS to US/Canada numbers, on Wi-Fi.
- **Calls:** Unlimited free calls to US/Canada numbers, on Wi-Fi.
- **Data:** Free Flex plan includes "data for select apps" (email, navigation, rideshare). Full data is a paid upgrade ($5/mo for 1GB, etc.).
- **Signup:** Email + US area code selection. No SSN/ID required.
- **Catch:** Ad-supported (ads in app). Number may be recycled if inactive for 30+ days — keep it active by sending a SMS or making a call weekly.
- **Best for:** Cheapest possible real US number with SMS, accessible globally over Wi-Fi.
- **Verdict:** **TOP PICK** for the user's exact use case (SMS + wifi calling, no data needed).

### 2. Hushed — $1.99 to $7.99/month
- **URL:** https://hushed.com
- **Plans (verified from /pricing):**
  - **Prepaid:** $1.99–$7.99/month (intro price varies, often $1.99 first month, $7.99 standard). US/Canada number, unlimited talk & text over Wi-Fi or cellular data.
  - **Unlimited:** ~$9.99/month (unlimited US/Canada calls+texts)
  - **Pay-as-you-go:** $8.99/month (includes $1 international credit) — for international calling
- **Number type:** Real US/Canada numbers from Canadian CLEC partner — supported by US/Canada caller ID systems.
- **Wi-Fi calling:** Yes — app-based VoIP over Wi-Fi or cellular data. Works globally.
- **SMS:** Unlimited SMS/MMS to US/Canada.
- **Data:** N/A — runs over Wi-Fi or your existing data connection.
- **Signup:** Email only. No ID, no SSN, no contract.
- **Catch:** Area code availability is "select on signup" — popular codes may not be offered.
- **Verdict:** Solid #2 — paid but more reliable than TextNow, no ads, app is cleaner.

### 3. Burner (by Adhesion)
- **URL:** https://burnerapp.com
- **Plans:** Standard ~$4.99/month (1 line, unlimited calls/texts, Wi-Fi), Premium ~$11.99/month (3 lines, auto-reply, custom voicemail). Annual saves 50%.
- **Number type:** Real US numbers — Burner uses real carrier-grade VoIP numbers, identifiable as mobile in many CNAM dips.
- **Wi-Fi calling:** Yes — app-based, works over Wi-Fi or cellular.
- **SMS:** Unlimited SMS/MMS to US numbers.
- **Data:** N/A.
- **Signup:** US phone number required to verify, then app-based. Email + payment.
- **Catch:** Requires a primary US phone to verify on signup (used as the "carrier line"); best for users who already have a US mobile. Subscriptions via iOS App Store or Google Play.
- **Verdict:** Reliable, US-only, app-based. Good third choice.

## Tier 2 — US MVNO eSIM Plans (Real US Carrier Numbers)

These are real US carrier-grade eSIMs from MVNOs (resellers on T-Mobile/AT&T/Verizon). They include data, but you can ignore the data — the **number is a real US mobile number**, valid for SMS/calls.

### 4. Tello Mobile — from $5/month
- **URL:** https://tello.com (Cloudflare blocked our scrape but the site is live)
- **Plan:** Build-your-own from $5/month — 1GB data, unlimited talk & text, no contract, eSIM supported.
- **Network:** T-Mobile US (real NPAC-registered mobile number).
- **Wi-Fi calling:** Yes — T-Mobile supports native Wi-Fi calling on the eSIM. Works globally on Wi-Fi (T-Mobile "simple global" doesn't apply since you're not roaming on cell, but Wi-Fi calling works anywhere).
- **Signup:** Email, US address (can use a mail-forwarding address), eSIM delivered via QR. No SSN required.
- **Verdict:** Cheapest real US carrier eSIM with native Wi-Fi calling. Number is fully mobile and can be ported out.

### 5. US Mobile (eSIM) — from $5–$8/month
- **URL:** https://usmobile.com (Cloudflare blocked — verify pricing direct)
- **Plan:** Light Start plan ~$5–$8/month (1–2 GB, unlimited talk & text). Annual savings available.
- **Network:** T-Mobile or Verizon (your choice at signup).
- **Wi-Fi calling:** Yes — native carrier Wi-Fi calling.
- **Signup:** Email, US address, eSIM QR. Premium plan unlocks VoLTE and Wi-Fi roaming.
- **Verdict:** Real US carrier number, native Wi-Fi calling, cheap entry plan.

### 6. Ultra Mobile PayGo — $3/month
- **URL:** ultramobile.com/paygo (Cloudflare blocked — verify direct)
- **Plan:** $3/month prepaid — 100 min talk, 100 SMS, 100MB data. eSIM supported.
- **Network:** T-Mobile.
- **Wi-Fi calling:** Yes — native Ultra PayGo supports Wi-Fi calling (verify on signup).
- **Signup:** Email, address, eSIM QR. No SSN.
- **Verdict:** Cheapest "real carrier" plan. 100 SMS/month is enough if you only need SMS verification. More minutes/SMS at $0.01 each overage.

### 7. H2O Wireless — from $10/month
- **URL:** h2owireless.com
- **Plan:** $10/month 1GB unlimited talk+text (sometimes sale).
- **Network:** AT&T.
- **Wi-Fi calling:** Yes on Wi-Fi calling enabled phones (AT&T network supports it).
- **eSIM supported:** Yes via H2O app.
- **Verdict:** Real AT&T number, entry-level pricepoint.

## Tier 4 — VoIP Virtual Number Providers (NOT real US carrier numbers)

The user emphasized "real number USA based". The following are **VoIP virtual numbers**, not real US carrier numbers — they often fail SMS verification on platforms that distinguish VoIP (e.g., banks, WhatsApp, Telegram). Listed for completeness.

- **Skype Number** — VoIP US number, often rejected by SMS verifications
- **Google Voice** — VoIP US number, often rejected by some platforms (but accepted by many)
- **Twilio / Telnyx / Plivo numbers** — VoIP, often rejected
- **Vonage, Bandwidth.com** — VoIP via their CLEC arm (some are classified as wireline)
